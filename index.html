Geovisualization Portfolio GEOG:3540

Choropleth Map

This choropleth map, classified using Jenk's natural breaks, visualizes the percentage of science and engineering Degrees in MD counties from 2009-2011. The overarching goal is to provide insight into the distribution of STEM degrees in this region and the correlation between degrees and educational institution locations. Data sources include NHGIS, the Maryland GIS Data Catalog and ACS.

Proportional Symbols Map

This proportional symbol map visualizes the locations of bison sightings documented by the Global Biodiversity Information Facility and is classified by year of observation; data ranges from 2000-2025. From this map, one can observe and make inferences from the spatial and temporal patterns that are simultaneously revealed. The data is sourced from GBIF.

Isarithmic Map

This isarithmic map visualizes the yearly total precipitation in Iowa to allow for a deeper understanding of spatial climatological patterns, prompting questions about other climate variables, such as temperature. The data is sourced from the Iowa Environmental Mesonet.

Bivariate Choropleth Map

This bivariate choropleth map visualizes the percentage of single male family households alongside the percentage of single female family households in Illinois counties in 2020. This exploration sheds light on economic and social issues such as mobility, poverty, and relationship networks as they relate to single parenthood. The data is sourced from NHGIS.

Flow Map

This flow map visualizes Divvy bikeshare flows in Chicago, IL, including the number of flows to and from each station (node) and the direction of each flow. This prompts discussion about bike demand, specifically who rents bikes and for what types, lengths, and directions of trips. Data is sourced from Divvy.

Final Project

This ArcGIS StoryMap uses interactive elements and maps, including an HVI raster and a hotspot analysis raster, to explain heat vulnerability in Portland, OR. The goal is to identify where heat-vulnerable population exist and the factors that contribute to their severity to encourage action from residents and policymakers. Data sources include USGS, NLCD, and ACS.
